# Editorial Audit — Market Intelligence Report

**Audit date:** Post–first draft  
**Scope:** All `docs/`, `data/`, and `report/`  
**Standard:** Premium founder/investor briefing; analyst-report tone.

---

## Findings Addressed

| Issue | Location | Change |
|-------|----------|--------|
| No single “Key Takeaways” for skimmers | Executive Summary | Added 5-bullet Key Takeaways at top; Bull/Bear/Recommendation callout. |
| Unsupported / generic phrasing | Monetization (“No product works without…”) | Replaced with specific rationale. |
| Typo (non-English character) | User Value Prop (“次要”) | Corrected to “secondary.” |
| Missing executive lead | Problem, Segments, Market, Monetization, Risks, Retailer, Data, Validation, MVP, Innovation, User Value | Added “In brief” or one-sentence executive lead where missing. |
| Repetition of “combined flow” / “validate first” | Multiple docs | Left as-is where necessary for clarity; tightened in Executive Summary and Go/No-Go. |
| Meta / filler lines | Risks (“Honest risk register…”) | Shortened to one line. |
| Report lacks distinct callouts | Report viewer | Styled blockquotes and strong-leading paragraphs as callouts; added Key Takeaways and Bull/Bear/Recommendation block on report page. |
| Inconsistent section breaks | Minor | Standardized --- usage. |

---

## Standards Applied

- **Executive-first:** Every long-form doc has an “In brief” or equivalent at the top.
- **Evidence:** Claims stay hedged (“plausible,” “partial,” “unvalidated”) where evidence is weak; sources cited for market data.
- **No generic startup language:** Removed phrases that could apply to any startup; kept product-specific conclusions.
- **Callouts:** Key findings, risks, and recommendations surfaced in Executive Summary and (where relevant) in report rendering.
- **Formatting:** Tables used for comparison and structure; consistent heading hierarchy; no orphan headers.

---

## Not Changed (Intentional)

- **Repetition of “combined flow” / “recommendation + best deal”:** Required for readers who jump to a single section; kept with light variation (“the wedge,” “combined flow”).
- **ASCII landscape map (Competitive):** Kept; labeled as conceptual.
- **Length of segment write-ups (Customer):** Structure is repetitive by design for scanability; executive lead added instead of cutting detail.
