# Go / No-Go Memo

**In brief:** Conditional yes—pursue if validation supports demand for the combined flow and we can build a large catalog and RAG legally. Best version: one flow (similar + best price), **large catalog**, **ML recommendation engine**, **significant RAG** (reviews, tasting notes, expert content), affiliate-first, trust-focused. Next step: interviews → data prototype (large catalog + RAG content) → concierge → build or pivot. No if validation fails, we can’t sustain catalog/RAG, or economics don’t work.

---

## Is This Worth Pursuing?

**Conditional yes.** Worth pursuing **if** validation supports that (a) a meaningful segment wants “similar cigars + best price” in one flow, and (b) we can get and maintain catalog and pricing data legally and sustainably. **Not** worth pursuing as a venture-scale bet without that validation; **yes** as a focused, wedge business (affiliate-led recommendation + deal platform) if evidence supports it.

---

## Under What Conditions?

| Condition | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **Validation first** | No big build until interviews and concierge (or equivalent) show users want the combined flow and will click through. |
| **Data without scraping** | Affiliate feeds, manual curation, or partnership only. Scraping is brittle and often against ToS. |
| **Trust by design** | Rank by price/fit, not commission; disclose affiliate; no pay-to-rank in core results. |
| **US-only for launch** | Regulation and retailer focus are US-specific; international adds complexity. |
| **Willingness to stay small** | This may be niche or affiliate-scale, not venture-scale. That’s OK if unit economics and lifestyle fit. |

---

## Most Credible Version of the Idea

**Product:** A single US-focused web product where the user enters a cigar they like and gets (1) a short list of similar cigars and (2) the best current price (and retailer link) for each, including the original. Monetization: affiliate. Optional later: subscription for “pro” recs, deal alerts, or dynamic picks (curated list, not we-ship).

**Positioning:** “Find cigars you’ll like. See the best price.” Unbiased, transparent, no pay-to-rank.

**Business type:** Affiliate-led referral with optional content and (later) subscription. Not a retailer; not inventory.

---

## What Evidence Supports It

- **Market:** US online cigar is a multi-billion-dollar segment and growing; discovery and price comparison are existing behaviors (forums, Cigar Sense, CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout).
- **Gap:** No single product clearly owns “recommendation + best deal” in one trusted flow.
- **Monetization:** Affiliate programs exist (5–15%); comparison sites operate on this model.
- **Feasibility:** Large catalog can be built from affiliate feeds, licensed or public sources, and enrichment; RAG from reviews, tasting notes, and expert content. Pricing from affiliate feeds. ML + RAG requires investment in ingestion, indexing, and retrieval quality.

**What we do not yet have:** Direct evidence that users want the **combined** flow more than using two tools, or that they’ll adopt a **new** entrant. Hence validation before build.

---

## What Would Make This a No

- **Validation:** Most users say they wouldn’t use it, or only want deal OR rec, not both.
- **Data:** We cannot get or maintain catalog and prices without scraping or violating ToS.
- **Trust:** Users refuse to use a third party for recommendation or price; they only trust retailer or forum.
- **Economics:** Affiliate conversion or commission is too low to support even a lean operation after realistic traffic.
- **Legal:** Counsel advises that our model creates unacceptable tobacco or affiliate risk in key states.
- **Strategic:** We decide we don’t want to run a niche/affiliate business and only want venture-scale; in that case, this idea is the wrong fit.

---

## If Yes: Exact Next Step

1. **Run 15–20 interviews** with US online cigar buyers (discovery or price-comparing segment). Ask about combined flow and trust.
2. **Build a data prototype:** Large catalog (affiliate/public/licensed where possible) and RAG-capable content (reviews, tasting notes); no scraping.
3. **Run a concierge test** with 10–20 users: manual “similar + best price” delivery; measure satisfaction and click-through.
4. **If results support it:** Build with large catalog, ML recommendation engine, and significant RAG; single flow, affiliate links. Launch; measure funnel and conversions.
5. **If results don’t support combined:** Test deal-only or recommendation-only; or stop and document learnings.

> **Do not** invest in subscription or app until the core flow (large catalog + ML + RAG) is validated and we have evidence of click-through and (if possible) attributed purchases.
