# Customer Segmentation

**In brief:** Seven segments; primary MVP targets are enthusiasts (discovery + price) and bargain hunters (price). Discovery-oriented and subscription-friendly matter for recommendation-first and later subscription. Brand-loyal and gift buyers are weak or secondary fit. All analysis is online buyers only.

---

## Segment Overview

| Segment | Size (relative) | Discovery need | Price sensitivity | Trust in 3P rec | Likely monetization fit |
|---------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Enthusiasts / hobbyists | Medium | High | Medium | Medium–high | Affiliate, possible subscription |
| Occasional premium buyers | Medium | Medium | Low–medium | Medium | Affiliate |
| Gift buyers | Large | Low | Low–medium | Low | Affiliate (one-off) |
| Bargain hunters | Medium | Low–medium | High | Medium | Affiliate (high intent) |
| Brand-loyal buyers | Medium | Low | Low | Low | Weak fit |
| Discovery-oriented / variety seekers | Medium | High | Medium | High | Affiliate, subscription |
| Subscription-friendly | Small–medium | High | Medium | High | Subscription, affiliate |

---

## Segment Detail

### 1. Experienced cigar hobbyists / enthusiasts

**Description:** Regular smokers (e.g., multiple times per month); follow brands, reviews, and community; often buy online and in B&M.

**Buying behavior:** Mix of repeat purchases and experimentation; read Halfwheel, Cigar Aficionado, forums; buy boxes and samplers.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** High interest in “what’s like X” and “what should I try next”; use forums and recommendation tools.

**Price comparison:** Care about value; will check deals and compare for big purchases; may still have a favorite retailer.

**Trust:** More likely to trust independent or expert-backed recommendation (e.g., Cigar Sense); skeptical of obvious retailer bias.

**Monetization:** Strong affiliate fit; possible premium for “pro” recommendations or deal alerts. **Primary target for MVP.**

---

### 2. Occasional premium buyers

**Description:** Smoke occasionally (e.g., celebrations, weekends); buy premium; not deep in community.

**Buying behavior:** Fewer transactions; may buy by brand name or recommendation from friend/salesperson.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** Open to “if you like X, try Y” if simple and credible; less likely to dig through forums.

**Price comparison:** Moderate; will use a comparison if easy; not the main driver.

**Trust:** Need clear, simple value; may prefer a known retailer unless tool is obviously helpful.

**Monetization:** Affiliate; one-off or occasional use. **Secondary for MVP.**

---

### 3. Gift buyers

**Description:** Buying for someone else; may not smoke themselves.

**Buying behavior:** Once or few times per year; often “nice box” or “something like what they like.”

**Discovery vs. substitution:** “Similar to X” is relevant if they know the recipient’s preference; else need simple guidance (strength, occasion).

**Price comparison:** Often less price-sensitive; care about perceived quality and presentation.

**Trust:** Need simple, safe recommendation; less concern about independence.

**Monetization:** Affiliate; volume can be high but low repeat. **Use case for content and simple flows; not core MVP.**

---

### 4. Bargain hunters

**Description:** Focus on deals, coupons, and lowest price; may be enthusiasts or occasional buyers.

**Buying behavior:** Check deal sites, retailer emails, comparison tools; buy when price is right.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** “Similar” matters if it unlocks cheaper alternatives; otherwise focus on price for a known item.

**Price comparison:** Core need; already use CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, or similar.

**Trust:** Will use comparison if it consistently shows better prices; sensitive to perceived bias.

**Monetization:** Strong affiliate fit; high intent. **Primary for deal-first wedge.**

---

### 5. Brand-loyal buyers

**Description:** Mostly buy one or few brands; know what they want.

**Buying behavior:** Repeat purchases; may stick to one retailer or shop around for that brand only.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** Low; “similar” is less relevant.

**Price comparison:** Possible for their brand; not discovery-driven.

**Trust:** Low need for recommendation; may still use price comparison.

**Monetization:** Affiliate only when they compare; **weak fit for recommendation-led product.**

---

### 6. Discovery-oriented / variety seekers

**Description:** Enjoy trying new cigars; value novelty and curation.

**Buying behavior:** Samplers, COTM clubs, recommendations from reviews/forums.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** High; “like X, try Y” and “what’s new” are key.

**Price comparison:** Medium; care about value but also quality of recommendation.

**Trust:** Will try new tools if recommendations feel credible and diverse.

**Monetization:** Affiliate; strong fit for subscription (dynamic picks). **Primary for recommendation-first or subscription wedge.**

---

### 7. Subscription-friendly users

**Description:** Overlap with enthusiasts and discovery-oriented; willing to pay for curated monthly picks.

**Buying behavior:** Already in or considering cigar-of-the-month; value convenience and curation.

**Discovery vs. substitution:** High; open to “personalized” or “dynamic” subscription based on taste.

**Price comparison:** May still want to see value vs. buying singles/boxes elsewhere.

**Trust:** Need transparency on how picks are chosen; sensitive to “we just push overstock.”

**Monetization:** Subscription revenue; can layer on affiliate for non-sub purchases. **Later-phase; validate after core rec/deal product.**

---

## Summary: Who Cares Most?

| Value driver | Segments that care most |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| “Similar to X” recommendations | Enthusiasts, discovery-oriented, subscription-friendly, occasional (if simple) |
| Best price / deal finding | Bargain hunters, enthusiasts, occasional (when buying boxes) |
| One place for both | Unvalidated; likely enthusiasts and bargain hunters if executed well |
| Subscription / curated picks | Discovery-oriented, subscription-friendly (later) |

**Recommended MVP focus:** Enthusiasts and discovery-oriented buyers for recommendation; bargain hunters and enthusiasts for deal-finding. Validate whether “both in one place” beats “two tools” for these segments.
