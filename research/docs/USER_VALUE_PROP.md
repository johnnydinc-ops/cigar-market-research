# Why This Adds Value for the Average Buyer

**In brief:** We reduce time, uncertainty, and overpaying for online buyers who care about discovery and/or price. Value is strongest for enthusiasts and bargain hunters; it depends on recommendation quality, price accuracy, and trust that we don’t steer by commission. Caveat: we only add value if our logic and data are good enough and trusted.

---

## What We Solve Better Than Browsing Retailer Sites Directly

| User goal | Retailer-only experience | With our product (if we execute) |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| “What’s similar to this cigar?” | Search same site; use filters; maybe get “customers also bought.” No cross-retailer “similar” view. | One input (cigar they like) → similar options across a taxonomy → then see those options with price across retailers. |
| “Where is this cigar cheapest right now?” | Check one retailer; maybe open a few tabs. | One place: see multiple retailers, delivered cost, stock. |
| “I want to try something new but don’t want to waste money.” | Samplers (generic) or COTM (curated but not “like what I like”). | “Like X” recommendations tied to real inventory and price; lower risk than blind samplers. |
| “I don’t want to overpay.” | Rely on one retailer’s promos or hunt coupons. | Comparison of current price (and promos) across retailers in one view. |

**Caveat:** We only add value if our “similar” logic and our price data are **good enough** and **trusted**. If we’re wrong or gamed, we don’t beat retailer direct.

---

## What We Reduce

| Friction | What we reduce |
|----------|----------------|
| **Time** | Fewer tabs and sites to check for “similar” and “best price.” |
| **Uncertainty** | “Is this really similar?” and “Am I getting a good price?” — we provide a structured answer (with clear limits). |
| **Analysis paralysis** | One starting point (cigar you like) → narrowed set of options with prices, instead of open-ended search. |
| **Overpaying** | Visibility of prices across retailers; user can choose lowest or best value. |
| **Lack of discovery** | “Similar to X” plus “where to buy” in one flow, instead of forum → then search elsewhere. |

---

## Who Feels This Most Strongly

| Segment | Value felt most |
|---------|------------------|
| **Enthusiasts** who explore and care about both “what to try” and “what it costs” | High. They already use multiple tools; one combined flow saves time and reduces doubt. |
| **Bargain hunters** who care about price for a known or similar cigar | High for price; medium for “similar” (only if it unlocks cheaper alternatives). |
| **Discovery-oriented** buyers who want to try new things | High for “similar”; medium for price (nice-to-have). |
| **Occasional** buyers who don’t want to become experts | Medium. They benefit if the UX is simple and the rec feels credible. |
| **Gift buyers** | Medium for “similar to what they like”; lower for deep price comparison. |
| **Brand-loyal** buyers who rarely explore | Low. They care about price for their brand only; recommendation is secondary. |

**“Average” buyer:** In the US online cigar market, “average” mixes occasional and enthusiast. The **average buyer who already shops online and cares a bit about discovery or price** gets: less effort, less overpay risk, and a single path from “I like X” to “here are options and where to buy.” The **average buyer who only ever buys one brand from one site** gets little; we’re not for them first.

---

## Where Value Comes From (Ranked for Our Product)

1. **Convenience** — One place for “similar” + “best price” instead of multiple tools. Strong if we execute.
2. **Deal-finding** — Not overpaying; seeing delivered cost. Strong for price-sensitive users.
3. **Discovery** — “Similar to X” with purchase path. Strong for explorers; depends on recommendation quality.
4. **Trust** — Unbiased comparison and transparent affiliate. Builds over time; differentiator if we hold the line.
5. **Novelty** — “Cool tool” is weak retention; utility is what brings them back.

---

## What Would Make the Average Buyer Not Care

- They only buy one brand from one retailer → no discovery or comparison need.
- They don’t trust a new third party → they stay with retailer or forum.
- Our “similar” is wrong or generic → they ignore recommendations.
- Our prices are stale or wrong → they stop trusting and leave.
- They prefer the “hunt” (forums, multiple sites) → our convenience is not valued.

---

## Summary (Founder Takeaway)

We add value when we **reduce time, uncertainty, and overpaying** for **online buyers who care about discovery and/or price**. Value is strongest for **enthusiasts and bargain hunters**, and for **discovery-oriented** users. It depends on **recommendation quality** and **price accuracy**, and on **trust** that we’re not steering by commission. The “average” buyer we want is the one who already looks for “what to try” or “best price” somewhere; we make that search easier and more reliable in one place.
