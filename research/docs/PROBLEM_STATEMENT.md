# Problem Statement

**In brief:** Discovery and deal-finding each have partial solutions (recommendation tools vs. price-comparison sites); the open question is whether combining them in one trusted flow is meaningfully better for a large enough segment.

---

## The Actual User Problem

**In one sentence:** US cigar buyers who want to discover similar cigars and find the best price face a fragmented experience—recommendation lives in one set of tools or communities, and price comparison in another, with no single trusted place that does both in one flow.

**Who experiences it:** Online cigar buyers in the US who (a) care about what to try next (discovery/substitution) and/or (b) care about not overpaying (deal-finding, comparison). Most acutely: enthusiasts and regular buyers who purchase multiple times per year and have some openness to trying new sticks rather than only rebuying the same brand.

**Who does not experience it (or experiences it weakly):** Buyers who only ever buy one brand from one retailer; one-off gift buyers who need a box of “something nice”; buyers who prioritize in-person experience (B&M, lounge) over online research.

---

## How They Solve It Today

| Need | Current workaround | Friction |
|------|--------------------|----------|
| “What’s similar to X?” | Forums (FOH, Reddit), review sites (Halfwheel, Cigar Aficionado), recommendation tools (Cigar Sense, Cigar AI, Stogie Match), retailer filters (Holt’s Cigar Finder). | Scattered: recommendation is separate from purchase. No single “like X, try these, and here’s where they’re cheapest.” |
| “Where is X cheapest right now?” | Price-comparison sites (CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, Cigarsy), or manually checking retailer sites and coupon pages. | Multiple tabs, different UX, coupon codes and shipping rules vary; trust in “best price” depends on site. |
| “I want to try new things without wasting money.” | Samplers, cigar-of-the-month clubs, forum recommendations, retailer “if you like” copy. | Samplers are often generic; COTM is curated but not personalized to “similar to what I like”; forum advice is noisy and not transactional. |
| “I don’t want to overpay.” | Deal threads, retailer emails, comparison sites. | Deal fatigue; not personalized to the specific cigar or box the user wants. |

---

## Current Shopping Journey (Simplified)

1. **Awareness** — Word of mouth, magazine, forum, retailer site, or review site.
2. **Consideration** — “What else is like this?” → forums, review sites, or dedicated recommendation tools.
3. **Where to buy** — Either stick with a known retailer or check comparison sites / multiple retailer sites.
4. **Purchase** — On a single retailer site (often the one they already trust or the one that had a coupon).

**Frictions:**

- **Discovery friction:** Recommendation and purchase are disconnected. User may get “try Y and Z” from Cigar Sense or a forum, then must search for Y and Z on retailer or comparison sites.
- **Comparison friction:** No unified view of “this cigar + similar cigars + best price for each” in one place. User either trusts one comparison site or does manual checks.
- **Trust friction:** Recommendation engines can be perceived as biased (retailer-owned) or narrow (inventory-limited). Price-comparison sites are affiliate-funded; users may wonder if “best” is gamed.
- **Convenience friction:** Power users can assemble the picture across multiple tools; casual or time-poor buyers may not bother and default to one retailer.

---

## Which Part of the Problem Is Painful Enough to Matter?

| Problem slice | Pain level (hypothesis) | Evidence |
|---------------|-------------------------|----------|
| “I don’t know what to try next.” | **Medium–high** for enthusiasts. | Forums and recommendation tools exist and are used; Cigar Sense positions on “discovery”; subscription clubs exist. |
| “I don’t know where this cigar is cheapest.” | **Medium** for price-sensitive or high-AOV buyers. | CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, Cigarsy exist and are sustained; affiliate programs suggest retailers accept comparison. |
| “I want both in one place, fast.” | **Unvalidated.** | No direct evidence yet; inferred from fragmentation. **Needs validation.** |
| “I don’t trust recommendations from retailers.” | **Partial.** | Cigar Sense differentiates on independence; implies some trust concern. Magnitude unknown. |

> **Sharp conclusion:** The problem is **real but split**. Discovery and deal-finding each have partial solutions. The open question is whether **combining** them in one trusted flow is meaningfully better than the status quo, and for which segment. That should drive validation.
