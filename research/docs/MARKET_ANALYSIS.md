# Market and Demand Analysis

**In brief:** US cigar ~$13B+ total; online ~$3.5B+ and growing. Discovery and price sensitivity exist; repeat use of a single tool is unknown. Business type: referral/affiliate-first, not retailer or marketplace. Enough to support a niche or affiliate business; venture-scale would require material share or expansion.

---

| Metric | Value | Source note |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| US cigar and cigarillos market (2024) | ~$13.07–13.3B | Grand View Research; industry reports |
| Projected (2033) | ~$24.97B | CAGR ~7.5% |

**Conclusion:** Online is a meaningful and growing slice of a large market. Our target is the **online buyer** subset, not the whole cigar market.

---

## Product Mix (Context)

- **Mass vs. premium:** Mass has larger share (~77%); premium is growing among affluent consumers.
- **Flavored vs. unflavored:** Flavored ~53.5% (can vary by segment; premium skews unflavored).
- **Channel:** Offline (specialty, lounges) still dominant by volume; **online is the fastest-growing channel.**

Implication: Our product is most relevant to **online buyers** of **premium or value premium** cigars who care about discovery and/or price. Mass/flavored may be a different segment (e.g., different retailers, different discovery needs).

## Buyer Behavior (Inference and Assumptions)

| Behavior | What we assume / infer | Evidence level |
|----------|------------------------|----------------|
| Frequency | Enthusiasts buy multiple times per year; occasional buyers fewer. | General; not US-cigar-specific survey. |
| Brand loyalty | Some buyers are loyal to one brand/retailer; others explore. | Forums and variety of samplers/Clubs imply exploration. |
| Discovery | “What to try next” is served by forums, COTM, review sites, recommendation tools. | Existence of Cigar Sense, Cigar AI, clubs, Halfwheel. |
| Price sensitivity | Meaningful segment shops for deals; comparison sites exist. | CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, Cigarsy; affiliate programs. |
| Repeat use of a single tool | Unknown. | No data; could be one-off (search → buy) or recurring. |

**Conclusion:** Discovery and price matter for at least a subset. We have **not** validated size of that subset or willingness to use a new combined tool. That belongs in validation.

---

## Role of Discovery vs. Habit

- **Discovery:** Samplers, cigar-of-the-month, “if you like X” recommendations, forum threads, Top 25 lists. Implies non-trivial share of demand is “what should I try?”
- **Habit / loyalty:** Repeat purchases of same brand/size; loyalty to one retailer. Implies another share does not need discovery.

Our product is **not** for the pure habit segment. It is for the **discovery-inclined** and/or **price-comparing** segment. Size of that segment is an **open question**.

---

## Role of Discounts and Promotions

- Retailers run daily deals, email promos, “Make Me An Offer,” rewards programs.
- Comparison and coupon sites exist and are monetized via affiliate.
- Implication: **Price and promotion matter** for a material share of demand; our deal-finding angle is aligned with existing behavior.

---

## Repeat-Use Pattern for Our Service

- **Scenario A:** User returns each time they’re considering a purchase (utility; may be occasional).
- **Scenario B:** User bookmarks and uses weekly/monthly (habit; stickier).
- **Scenario C:** User uses once and never returns (one-off; we depend on SEO and volume).

We do **not** know which dominates. If C dominates, the business is more like **lead-gen / affiliate** with high volume and SEO. If A or B, we can aim for **product loyalty** and possibly subscription. Validation should probe this.

---

## What Kind of Business Is This?

| Lens | Implication |
|------|-------------|
| **Utility** | “I need the best price for this cigar” or “what’s like X?” — use when needed. |
| **Marketplace** | We don’t hold inventory; we refer. So we’re **referral / affiliate**, not true marketplace. |
| **Media** | Content (reviews, guides) + comparison + recs could be media-like; monetize via affiliate and possibly ads. |
| **Niche enthusiast platform** | Community, trust, depth for cigar buyers; monetize via affiliate, subscription, or sponsorship. |

**Conclusion:** This is **referral/affiliate-first**, with optional **media** (content) and **enthusiast platform** (trust, community). It is **not** a retailer or a classic two-sided marketplace. That shapes economics and positioning.

---

## Useful Conclusions (No Fluff)

1. **Market size:** Online US cigar is billions and growing; enough to support a niche business; venture-scale would require capturing a material share of that online demand or expanding scope.
2. **Discovery and price:** Both exist as behaviors; combined “rec + deal” is the wedge, not “discovery” or “price” alone.
3. **Segment focus:** Enthusiasts and price-sensitive online buyers are the likely early adopters; gift and one-off buyers are volume but not core.
4. **Repeat use:** Unknown; validate. If one-off dominates, model as SEO-driven affiliate; if repeat, aim for product and maybe subscription.
5. **Business type:** Affiliate-led referral with optional content and subscription; not inventory or marketplace.
