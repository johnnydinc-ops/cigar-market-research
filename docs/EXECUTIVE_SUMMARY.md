## The Startup Idea

A **US-only platform** for cigar buyers that:

1. Lets a user enter a cigar they already like.
2. **Recommends similar cigars** (flavor, strength, body, wrapper, origin, size, price band).
3. **Finds the best current deal** for that cigar and close substitutes across US online retailers.
4. Makes **discovery, comparison, and buying** easier in one flow.

Directions researched: matching marketplace, deal engine, recommendation engine, dynamic subscription, advertising for brands/retailers, affiliate/lead-gen.

---

## Market Need

- **Discovery:** Many buyers want “what to try next” or “what’s like X.” Today they use forums, review sites (Halfwheel, Cigar Aficionado), or dedicated tools (Cigar Sense, Cigar AI, Stogie Match)—**separate** from where they buy.
- **Price:** Many want the best price. They use comparison sites (CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, Cigarsy) or check multiple retailers.
- **Gap:** No single product clearly owns **“similar cigars + best price” in one trusted flow.** Recommendation and deal-finding are fragmented.

**Customer pain:** Friction from using multiple tools and tabs; uncertainty about “similar” and “best price”; some distrust of retailer-only recommendations.

---

## Is the Opportunity Real?

**Partially.** The pain is **real but split**: discovery and deal-finding each have partial solutions. The **open question** is whether combining them in one product is meaningfully better than the status quo for a large enough segment. Evidence so far:

- **Supportive:** Online US cigar market is large and growing (~$13B+ total; online ~$3.5B+); comparison and recommendation tools exist and are used; affiliate programs exist (5–15%); no clear category leader for “rec + deal” combined.
- **Unvalidated:** Whether users want the **combined** flow; whether they’ll adopt a **new** entrant; retention vs. one-off use.

**Verdict:** Opportunity is **plausible but not proven.** It looks **niche- or affiliate-viable**; **venture-scale** would require capturing a material share of online demand or expanding scope. Recommendation: **proceed narrowly**—validate first, then build a focused MVP.

---

## Target Customer

**Primary:** US online cigar buyers who (a) care about discovery (“what’s like X?”) and/or (b) care about not overpaying. Most likely: **enthusiasts** and **bargain hunters**; **discovery-oriented** and **subscription-friendly** for later phases.

**Secondary:** Occasional premium buyers, gift buyers (for simple flows).

**Not primary:** Buyers who only ever buy one brand from one retailer; pure B&M buyers.

---

## Biggest Risks

1. **Weak or unvalidated pain** — Combined flow may not be important enough; users may prefer two separate tools.
2. **Retailer and data dependence** — Affiliate and data access can change; scraping is not a sustainable strategy.
3. **Affiliate economics** — Conversion or commission may not support the business at scale.
4. **Trust** — If we’re seen as steering by commission, we lose users.
5. **Incumbent response** — CigarFinder or others could add strong “similar” and own the combined space.

---

## Venture-Scale vs. Niche vs. Affiliate

| Outcome | Assessment |
|---------|------------|
| **Venture-scale** | Possible only if we capture a material share of online cigar demand or expand (e.g., subscription, international). Not the base case. |
| **Niche but viable** | **Likely** if validation passes: a sustainable affiliate + content business serving enthusiasts and deal-seekers. |
| **Affiliate-style viable** | **Very likely** if we can drive qualified traffic and convert; many comparison sites run on this model. |
| **Not compelling** | If validation shows no demand for combined flow, or we can’t get data or trust. |

**Current assessment:** **Niche but viable** or **affiliate-style viable** with a clear wedge (combined rec + deal; trust). **Venture-scale** is optional and would require proof of retention and scale.

---

## Recommendation

**Proceed narrowly.**

- **Do not** rush into building a full product. **Do** run validation: interviews (15–20), data prototype (large catalog + RAG-capable content where possible), concierge test (10–20 users).
- **If validation supports it:** Build with a **large catalog** from the start and an **ML recommendation engine** backed by **significant RAG** (reviews, tasting notes, expert content) so “similar” is grounded in real cigar knowledge. One flow (“cigar I like” → similar cigars → best price for each → affiliate links); 5–10 retailers. Monetize via affiliate; no subscription or sponsored results in v1.
- **If validation is weak:** Pivot to deal-only or recommendation-only, or stop and document learnings.
- **Differentiate** on **trust** (unbiased ranking; clear disclosure) and **UX** (one flow). Do not rely on scraping; use affiliate, feeds, manual curation, or partnership.
- **Position** as “Find cigars you’ll like. See the best price.” US-only; no inventory; we refer, we don’t sell.

**Stop conditions:** Validation shows most users wouldn’t use it; we can’t build or maintain a large catalog and RAG legally/sustainably; affiliate economics don’t work; legal risk is unacceptable.
