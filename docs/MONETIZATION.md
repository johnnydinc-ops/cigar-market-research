# Monetization Analysis

**In brief:** Affiliate (and lead-gen) first—aligns with “best deal” narrative, multiple retailers have programs, low operational complexity. Sponsorship and subscription later, after trust and traction. Conflicts of interest: rank by price/fit, not commission; disclose; label sponsored.

---

## Option Overview

| Option | Feasibility | User trust alignment | Margin profile | Operational complexity | When |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|------------------------|------|
| Affiliate commissions | High | OK if unbiased and disclosed | Variable; 5–15% of AOV | Low | **First** |
| Lead gen / referral fee | High | Same as affiliate | Similar | Low | With affiliate |
| Retailer sponsorship / promoted placement | Medium | **Risk:** can undermine “best price” | Higher per placement | Medium | After trust established |
| Premium recommendations / paywall | Low–medium | OK if free tier is useful | Recurring | Medium | Later |
| Dynamic cigar subscription | Medium | OK if transparent curation | Recurring + margin | High | Later |
| B2B advertising (brands / retailers) | Medium | Risk if it biases recs | CPM or fixed | Medium | Later |
| Data / analytics (B2B) | Low | Privacy/compliance | Per-seat or license | High | Much later |
| Membership (deal alerts, pro recs) | Medium | OK if value is clear | Recurring | Medium | After traction |

---

## 1. Affiliate commissions

**How it works:** User clicks our link to retailer; we earn commission (5–15%) on qualifying purchase.

**Feasibility:** **High.** Multiple US retailers have programs (Famous up to 15%, Cigar Country 10%, Cigars International 5–7%, etc.). Cookie windows 30–90 days.

**Trust:** Users accept affiliate if (a) we disclose, (b) “best price” or “best match” is not gamed by commission. We should order by price or fit, not by payout.

**Margin:** Depends on AOV and rate. Example: 8% on $120 AOV = $9.60 per conversion. Volume and conversion rate drive revenue.

**Complexity:** Low. Join programs; use correct links; comply with state restrictions (e.g., CI).

**When:** **First.** Affiliate is the only model that matches our value prop (we earn when they buy) without inventory or fulfillment.

---

## 2. Lead generation / referral

**How it works:** Same as affiliate in practice (we send traffic; we get paid per sale or per lead).

**Feasibility:** High; often same programs as affiliate.

**Trust:** Same as affiliate.

**When:** Launch with affiliate; optimize conversion.

---

## 3. Retailer sponsorship / promoted placements

**How it works:** Retailers or brands pay for featured placement (e.g., “sponsored” in results or “featured deal”).

**Feasibility:** Medium. Requires sales and retailer interest; some may resist if we’re seen as “best price” tool.

**Trust:** **Risk.** If users think we favor payers over true best price, trust drops. Mitigation: clear “Sponsored” labels; keep organic results unbiased.

**Margin:** Potentially higher per impression than affiliate, but volume uncertain.

**When:** **After** we have traffic and proof we drive sales. Do not launch with this as primary.

---

## 4. Premium recommendations / paywall

**How it works:** Free: basic “similar” and comparison. Paid: deeper personalization, saved preferences, “pro” recs, or more retailers.

**Feasibility:** Low–medium. Cigar Sense already charges for advice; we’d need a clear reason to pay (e.g., better UX, or rec + deal together).

**Trust:** Fine if free tier is genuinely useful and paid is clearly “more,” not “same with paywall.”

**When:** **Later.** Validate that users value the free product first; then test willingness to pay.

---

## 5. Dynamic cigar subscription

**How it works:** User sets taste profile; we (or a partner) send monthly picks that match, with possible price guarantee or deal.

**Feasibility:** Medium. Fulfillment is hard (we don’t hold inventory); we could partner with a retailer or white-label. Margin from markup or rev-share.

**Trust:** High if we’re transparent about how picks are chosen and don’t just push overstock.

**Complexity:** High (inventory, shipping, PACT, etc.). Easier as **curated list + affiliate** (“your monthly picks; buy here”) than as we-ship.

**When:** **Later.** After we have recommendation and deal product working; then test “dynamic subscription” as curated list with affiliate links.

---

## 6. B2B advertising (brands / newer retailers)

**How it works:** Brands or smaller retailers pay for visibility (e.g., “discover these brands,” “retailer spotlight”).

**Feasibility:** Medium. Depends on our traffic and demo; sales effort required.

**Trust:** **Risk.** If editorial or recs are influenced, we lose credibility. Keep ads separate from “best price” and “similar cigars” logic.

**When:** After we have audience; keep strict separation from core utility.

---

## 7. Data / analytics (B2B)

**How it works:** Sell aggregated insights (trends, pricing, demand) to retailers or brands.

**Feasibility:** Low for a long time. Need scale and clear product; privacy and contracts.

**When:** **Much later**, if at all.

---

## 8. Membership (deal alerts, pro recs)

**How it works:** Free: basic comparison and recs. Paid: early deal alerts, saved preferences, “best deal” alerts for saved cigars.

**Feasibility:** Medium. Depends on willingness to pay; unknown for cigar buyers.

**Trust:** OK if free tier is strong and paid is clearly extra value.

**When:** After traction; A/B test paywall vs. free-only.

---

## Best First Monetization Model

**Recommendation: Affiliate (and lead-gen) first.**

- No inventory, no fulfillment.
- Aligns with “we help you find the best deal” (we earn when they buy).
- Multiple retailers already have programs.
- Build trust by ranking by price/fit, not by commission; disclose affiliate clearly.

---

## Subscription: Early or Later?

**Later.** Reasons:

- Users need to trust that our free comparison and recommendation are unbiased before paying.
- Subscription clubs already exist for **product** (COTM); we’d be selling **recommendation + deal** or **dynamic curation**. That’s unvalidated.
- Better sequence: launch free (affiliate-funded) → prove value → then test subscription or membership.

---

## Conflicts of Interest

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Steering users to higher-commission retailers | Order results by price (or relevance), not commission; publish policy. |
| Sponsored results biasing “best” | Label “Sponsored”; keep organic algorithm independent. |
| Brand ads influencing recommendations | Keep ad product separate from “similar cigars” and “best price” logic. |

---

## Summary

- **First:** Affiliate (and lead-gen). Disclose; don’t game “best” by commission.
- **Next:** Optional retailer sponsorship with clear labeling, after we have traffic.
- **Later:** Premium features, membership, or dynamic subscription once value is proven.
- **Avoid early:** Paywalling core utility; letting sponsorship dictate ranking.
