# Research Plan: US Cigar Buyer Platform

## Objective

Determine whether a US-only platform that combines cigar recommendation (“if you like X, try Y”), deal-finding across retailers, and easier discovery is worth building—and if so, what wedge to pursue and what MVP to build.

---

## Key Questions

| # | Question | Why it matters |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Who exactly has the pain (discovery, substitution, deal-finding) and how acute is it? | Validates problem–solution fit. |
| 2 | How do US cigar buyers choose and buy today? What frictions exist? | Defines current journey and gaps. |
| 3 | Is anyone already doing “recommendation + best deal” well in one place? | Defines competitive gap vs. table stakes. |
| 4 | What data is needed, where does it come from, and is it obtainable legally/sustainably? | Feasibility of product and moat. |
| 5 | Can affiliate/lead-gen economics support the business at scale? | Monetization viability. |
| 6 | Would users trust a third party for recommendations and deal comparison? | Adoption and retention. |
| 7 | Is this venture-scale, niche-viable, or affiliate-only viable? | Scope of opportunity. |

---

## Research Approach

1. **Problem-first**  
   Define the actual user problem and current workarounds before solution design.

2. **Evidence over narrative**  
   Separate facts (sourced), assumptions (stated), and inferences (labeled). Flag weak evidence.

3. **Competitive reality check**  
   Map existing recommendation tools, price-comparison sites, subscriptions, and communities. Identify true gaps.

4. **Retailer and data realism**  
   Assess major US online retailers, data sources, and ethical/legal constraints. No reliance on brittle or questionable scraping.

5. **Monetization and unit economics**  
   Model affiliate, subscription, and advertising options with feasibility and trust implications.

6. **Validation before build**  
   Define what must be true for the idea to work and how to test those assumptions cheaply.

---

## Priority Unknowns

- **Primary:** Do enough US cigar buyers actively seek “similar cigars” and “best price” in one flow to justify a dedicated product?
- **Primary:** Is the gap “no one does both well” or “no one does both in one trusted place”?
- **Secondary:** Will retailers/affiliate programs support a comparison layer that sends users to the cheapest retailer?
- **Secondary:** How strong is brand loyalty vs. openness to discovery and substitution?
- **Tertiary:** Subscription willingness for “dynamic” recommendations vs. fixed cigar-of-the-month.

---

## Sources to Investigate

| Category | Sources |
|----------|--------|
| Market size & behavior | Grand View Research, Statista, industry reports (US cigar/cigarillos, online segment). |
| Recommendation tools | Cigar Sense, Cigar AI, Stogie Match, retailer finders (Holt’s, etc.). |
| Price comparison | CigarFinder, Cigar Price Scout, Cigarsy; how they work, coverage, UX. |
| Subscriptions | CigarClub, Holt’s COTM, Smoke Inn, Cigar Month Club; positioning and pricing. |
| Retailers | Famous Smoke, Cigars International, JR Cigars, Thompson, Gotham, Mike’s; affiliate programs, catalog depth. |
| Community & content | Halfwheel, Cigar Aficionado, FOH Forum, Reddit r/cigars; how recommendations happen today. |
| Data & compliance | Retailer ToS, affiliate terms, state tobacco restrictions, PACT Act implications for platform. |

## Success Criteria for Research

- Every strong claim is backed by a source or labeled as assumption/inference.
- Bull and bear cases are both argued; recommendation is explicit.
- If the idea is weak, the docs say so and explain why.
- If the idea is strong, the docs specify the wedge and MVP path.
- Outputs are founder- and investor-readable: no filler, no hype.
